MasterEvery Muscle.

Your complete guide to weight training. Pick a muscle group, explore every exercise, watch the perfect form, and understand exactly what's firing.

Chest

The pectorals are your primary pushing muscles. A well-developed chest requires hitting the muscle from multiple angles — upper, mid, and lower — while also focusing on the squeeze and stretch at different points in the range of motion.

Pectoralis Major Pectoralis Minor Serratus Anterior Anterior Deltoid (secondary)

Shoulders

The deltoids have three distinct heads — anterior, lateral, and posterior — each requiring different exercises to fully develop. Shoulder width, roundness, and rear delt thickness all depend on hitting every head consistently.

Anterior Deltoid Lateral Deltoid Posterior Deltoid Rotator Cuff

Back

The back is the most complex muscle group to train. Width comes from the lats, thickness from the traps and rhomboids, and lower back strength from the erector spinae. Pull from different angles to hit all regions.

Latissimus Dorsi Trapezius Rhomboids Erector Spinae Teres Major

Legs

The legs are your foundation. Quads, hamstrings, glutes, and calves each need dedicated work. The hamstrings are often underdeveloped — include both knee-flexion and hip-extension movements for complete development.

Quadriceps Hamstrings Glutes (Maximus / Medius / Minimus) Gastrocnemius Soleus

Biceps

The biceps have a long head (outer peak) and short head (inner thickness). The brachialis underneath pushes the bicep up and makes your arm thicker from the side. Train with both supinated and neutral grips.

Biceps Brachii (Long Head) Biceps Brachii (Short Head) Brachialis Brachioradialis

Triceps

Triceps make up ~⅔ of your upper arm. They have three heads — lateral (outer), medial (mid), and long (inner/largest). The long head only fully stretches when your arm is overhead, so include overhead extensions in your routine.

Lateral Head Medial Head Long Head
